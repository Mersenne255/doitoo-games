Cognitive Benefits

  • Divided Attention — Trains your brain to monitor and respond to multiple information sources at once
  • Task Switching — Improves the speed and accuracy of shifting between different cognitive tasks
  • Cognitive Flexibility — Builds the ability to adapt your thinking when task demands change rapidly
  • Multitasking Efficiency — Reduces the performance cost of handling concurrent activities
  • Executive Control — Strengthens top-down regulation of attention across competing goals
  • Response Prioritization — Sharpens your judgment on which task needs immediate action
  • Sustained Vigilance — Increases your capacity to stay alert across multiple task streams over time

How It Works

You manage multiple mini-tasks running simultaneously on screen. Each task requires a different type of response, and you must divide your attention to keep up with all of them at once. The challenge scales as more tasks are added or their pace increases.

The Mini-Games

🧮 Math Equations

Solve arithmetic problems using an on-screen number pad before time runs out. Operations get harder as difficulty rises. In a multitasking context, this forces your brain to maintain numerical reasoning while being interrupted by other tasks — training your ability to resume complex thought after context switches.

🔷 Matcher

Two shape cards appear in sequence. Decide whether they share a property (color, shape, border, or letter) based on the current rules. Under multitasking pressure, this trains rapid perceptual comparison and rule-based decision making while your attention is being pulled elsewhere.

🎯 Alike

A set of shape cards is displayed — find the odd one out based on active visual properties. As difficulty increases, more properties become relevant. In the multitasking environment, this challenges your visual scanning and pattern discrimination while competing tasks demand your focus.

🚀 Comet

Pilot a comet through a field of obstacles by holding to ascend and releasing to descend. Requires continuous motor control and spatial awareness. When combined with cognitive tasks, this trains your ability to maintain a steady physical response while your mind handles abstract problems elsewhere.
