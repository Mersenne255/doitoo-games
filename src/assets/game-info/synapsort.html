Cognitive Benefits

  • Cognitive Flexibility — Trains your ability to abandon a confirmed mental set and switch to a new one based on feedback
  • Set-Shifting — Strengthens the capacity to detect when rules change and adapt your strategy accordingly
  • Error Monitoring — Improves awareness of perseverative errors, where you repeat a strategy that no longer works
  • Abstract Reasoning — Builds skill in deducing hidden rules from limited feedback
  • Inhibitory Control — Reduces the tendency to persist with a previously successful but now incorrect approach
  • Feedback Utilization — Sharpens your ability to extract meaning from correct/incorrect signals and update behavior

How It Works

You see a card with a shape, color, and count. At the bottom are sorting piles, each with a reference card. You must sort the card to the correct pile — but the sorting rule is hidden. Is it shape? Color? Count? You figure it out through trial and error. After a streak of correct sorts, the rule silently switches. Detect the switch from feedback and adapt. Difficulty increases as rules switch more frequently, more piles appear, and cards become ambiguous.
