Cognitive Benefits

  • Inhibitory Control — Strengthens your ability to suppress automatic responses in favor of correct ones
  • Selective Attention — Trains you to focus on relevant stimulus features while ignoring distractors
  • Processing Speed — Improves how quickly you can identify and respond to color-based cues
  • Conflict Resolution — Builds the capacity to resolve interference between competing response options
  • Impulse Control — Reduces impulsive errors by training deliberate, accurate responding
  • Cognitive Inhibition — Sharpens the ability to block irrelevant information from influencing decisions
  • Response Accuracy — Encourages precise responses under time pressure and conflicting stimuli

How It Works

Color words are displayed in different ink colors, similar to the classic Stroop test. You must respond based on the ink color, not the word itself. When the word and color conflict, your brain must override the automatic urge to read the word, testing your inhibitory control under increasing speed and complexity.
